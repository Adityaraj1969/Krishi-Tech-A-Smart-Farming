Crop Categories

India's agricultural diversity spans four major seasons and countless varieties. Explore each category for detailed cultivation guides.

☀️ June – October

Kharif crops are sown at the beginning of the monsoon season. They require warm temperatures and abundant rainfall.

❄️ October – March

Rabi crops are sown in winter after monsoon rains. They need cool weather for growth and warm dry weather at maturity.

🌸 March – June

Zaid crops grow between Rabi and Kharif seasons. These short-duration crops are grown on river beds and irrigated land.

💰 Commercial Crops

Cash crops are grown primarily for commercial sale rather than subsistence. They form the backbone of India's textile and export industry.

🌸 Floriculture

India is a major producer of cut flowers and loose flowers. Floriculture is a rapidly growing segment of horticulture.

☕ Plantation Crops

Tea and Coffee are among India's most important plantation crops, grown primarily in the hilly regions of Assam, Darjeeling, and the Western Ghats.

Advanced Farming Techniques

Soil-less and technology-driven farming systems revolutionizing Indian agriculture for the 21st century.

Hydroponics

Grow crops in nutrient-rich water without soil. Ideal for urban farming, leafy greens, and herbs with 90% less water usage.

Aeroponics

Roots suspended in air and misted with nutrients. Fastest growth rates, highest yields, and minimal water consumption.
